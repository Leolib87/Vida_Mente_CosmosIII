Prehistory

Philosophy (Aristotle, Descartes, Kant, et al.)

Early thinkers laid foundations for theories of mind and reasoning.

Mathematics (Boole, Frege, Bayes)

Formal systems and probability theory shaped computational cognition.

Early Neuroscience (Hippocrates, Galen)

Initial insights into brain and nervous system function.

1860s

Psychophysics (Fechner, 1860)

Quantifies sensory perception, foundational for cognitive neuroscience.

1870s

Psychology Lab (Wundt, 1879)

First experimental psychology lab, emphasizing introspection.

1910s

Structuralism (Wundt, Titchener, 1910)

Analyzes mental structures via introspection.

1920s

EEG (Berger, 1929)

Non-invasive brain activity recording advances neuroscience.

Psychoanalysis (Freud, 1922)

Explores unconscious determinants of behavior.

1930s

Turing Machine & Incompleteness Theorem (1936)

Formalizes computation, foundational for AI.

Neural Modeling (Rashevsky, 1930s)

Early neural network models influence connectionism.

Cognitive Development (Piaget, 1930)

Describes stages of cognitive growth in children.

1940s

Cybernetics (Wiener, 1948)

Introduces feedback and self-organization in systems.

Cognitive Maps (Tolman, 1948)

Demonstrates mental representations in rats.

Hebbian Learning (Hebb, 1949)

Neurons that fire together wire together.

Information Theory (Shannon, 1948)

Formalizes data transmission and processing.

1950s

Birth of Cognitive Science (1956)

MIT Symposium unites Chomsky, Newell, Simon.

Artificial Intelligence (McCarthy, 1956)

Dartmouth Workshop launches symbolic AI.

Syntactic Structures (Chomsky, 1957)

Challenges behaviorism, shapes cognitive linguistics.

Perceptrons (Rosenblatt, 1958)

Early neural networks advance connectionist ideas.

Magical Number Seven (Miller, 1956)

Defines limits of short-term memory capacity.

1960s

Ecological Psychology (Gibson, 1966)

Affordances link perception to environmental interaction.

Perceptrons Critique (Minsky & Papert, 1969)

Exposes neural network limitations, causing AI winter.

Receptive Fields (Hubel & Wiesel, 1961)

Visual cortex discoveries advance neural processing research.

General Problem Solver (Newell & Simon, 1961)

Models human problem-solving with symbolic AI.

Human Memory Model (Atkinson & Shiffrin, 1968)

Formalizes multi-store model of human memory.

1970s

Autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela, 1972)

Self-organizing systems shape embodied cognition.

Judgments Under Uncertainty (Kahneman & Tversky, 1974)

Reveals cognitive biases, shaping behavioral economics.

Physical Symbol System Hypothesis (Newell & Simon, 1976)

Cognition as symbol manipulation advances symbolic AI.

Social Learning (Bandura, 1977)

Emphasizes observational learning in cognition.

Habituation in Aplysia (Kandel, 1970)

Reveals neural mechanisms of learning and memory.

Sloan Report (1978)

Formalizes cognitive science as a field.

1980s

Parallel Distributed Processing (Rumelhart & McClelland, 1986)

Revives connectionism with multilayer neural networks.

Cognitive Design (Norman, 1988)

Applies cognitive science to user-centered design.

Embodied Robotics (Brooks, 1986)

Representation-free AI via environmental interaction.

Modularity of Mind (Fodor, 1983)

Proposes distinct cognitive modules.

Levels of Analysis (Marr, 1982)

Framework for studying vision and cognition.

Adaptive Control of Thought (Anderson, 1983)

Models cognitive processes in problem-solving.

Lighthill Report (1973)

Critiques AI, contributing to AI winter.

Dynamic Systems Theory (Geert, Thelen, Smith, 1980s)

Applies nonlinear dynamics to cognition.

1990s

The Embodied Mind (Varela, Thompson, Rosch, 1991)

Foundational for enactivism and 4E cognition.

Bayesian Networks (Pearl, 1988)

Formalizes probabilistic reasoning in AI.

fMRI Development (Ogawa et al., 1990)

Non-invasive brain imaging revolutionizes neuroscience.

Cognition in the Wild (Hutchins, 1995)

Explores distributed cognition in real-world settings.

Recurrent Neural Networks (Elman, 1990)

Advances connectionist models of language processing.

Infant Cognition (Spelke, 1990s)

Reveals early cognitive capabilities in infants.

Mirror Neurons (Rizzolatti, 1996)

Links action and perception in neural processing.

Eyewitness Testimony (Loftus, 1996)

Reveals memory’s reconstructive nature.

Mind as Motion (Port & van Gelder, 1995)

Dynamic systems approach to cognition.

2000s

Rationality Debate (Kahneman vs. Gigerenzer, 2002)

Kahneman’s Nobel for biases; Gigerenzer defends ecological rationality.

Neural Plasticity (Kandel, 2000)

Nobel Prize for signal transduction in learning.

Cultural Cognition (Tomasello, 2005)

Explores social learning in primates.

Mind in Life (Thompson, 2007)

Extends enactivism with biological perspectives.

Probabilistic Models (Tenenbaum, 2000s)

Advances computational cognitive science.

Basic Emotions (Ekman, 1999)

Identifies universal emotions in cognition.

Complex Systems Approach (Orden, Turvey, 2000s)

Models cognition as emergent from interactions.

2010s

Predictive Processing (Clark & Friston, 2013)

Brain as prediction error minimizer.

Deep Learning Revolution (Hinton, 2012)

ImageNet win showcases deep neural networks.

AlphaGo Victory (2016)

DeepMind’s AI defeats Go champion Lee Sedol.

Place Cells (O’Keefe, 2014)

Nobel Prize for brain’s positioning system.

Computational Cognition (Gershman, 2015)

Advances probabilistic models of cognition.

Computational Cognitive Neuroscience (Kriegeskorte, 2018)

Integrates computational models with neuroscience.

Future

General AI & Consciousness

Pursuit of flexible AI and computational consciousness.

Quantum Computing & Convergent Tech

Potential to enhance cognitive modeling and AI.
